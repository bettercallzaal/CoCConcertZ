COC Concertz #4 — Joseph Goats live from Caracas, Venezuela

Joseph Goats is a Venezuelan singer-songwriter who has been using music for social coordination and indigenous community support since the early days of Web3 metaverse concerts. Based in Caracas, he recently rebranded from Jose to Joseph Goats to connect with English-language audiences while keeping his core identity rooted in Spanish-language music. He works closely with the Waha indigenous community in the Venezuelan Amazon and uses platforms like Giveth to fund real-world impact through his performances.

This set is three original songs performed live from Caracas on a rainy day, with the Venezuelan Amazon rainforest visible in the background. No Winter Slam was written for a DeFi contest about surviving harsh winters — it placed second and helped fund a trip to the Waha community. Kama is a rainy-day piece built to shift frequency, performed as rain falls in Caracas. Compass, known in Spanish as Brujula, draws from the Four Agreements and treats the concept of a smart contract as an indigenous philosophical model for coordination.

Between songs, Joseph shares context about the Waha community currently touring Venezuela, with a live Giveth fundraiser seeking $300 for a solar panel and fridge. He talks through his philosophy of finding your musical note as both a mathematical and personal process, connecting harmonic theory to mission and values. He closes with a call to action for April 22 Earth Day and the Live Earth activation.
